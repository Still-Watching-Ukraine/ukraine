Why this works

During a campaign, candidates are listening. They expect to hear from voters.

This is a chance to educate them, persuade them, and show them that Ukraine is an issue voters want to hear about. The more candidates hear from voters, the more likely they are to treat Ukraine as something they should talk about during the campaign.

If a candidate already supports Ukraine, they still need to hear that voters notice and value that support. Your message helps reinforce it and encourages them to say more.

Even candidates who lose can matter. They talk to voters, staff, donors, volunteers, local media, party activists, and other candidates. A question they hear now can show up later in debates, town halls, campaign emails, social posts, or future campaigns.

The goal is simple: make Ukraine harder to ignore.
